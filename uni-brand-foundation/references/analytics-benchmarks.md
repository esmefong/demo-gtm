# Uniswap — Analytics Benchmarks (demo outline)

What "good" looks like, per channel. Used to set targets for new content and to flag underperformance.

## 1. X (protocol account)
- **Floor (single post):** 80,000 impressions, 1.2% engagement.
- **Stretch:** 250,000 impressions, 3% engagement, replies from 3+ recognizable dev accounts.
- **Vanity to ignore:** like count without saves and replies.

## 2. X (devrel / individual contributor accounts)
- **Floor (thread):** 25,000 impressions on tweet 1, 35% read-through to tweet 4.
- **Stretch:** 80,000+ impressions, 50% read-through, 5+ implementer replies.

## 3. LinkedIn (institutional)
- **Floor:** 2,500 impressions, 1% engagement.
- **Stretch:** 8,000+ impressions, 2.5% engagement, comments from named institutional accounts.

## 4. Newsletter / governance digest
- **Floor:** 38% open rate, 3% CTR.
- **Stretch:** 52% open rate, 7% CTR, <0.2% unsubscribe.

## 5. Landing pages (hook spotlight, hackathon)
- **Floor:** 4% testnet-deployment conversion (developer traffic).
- **Stretch:** 9%+ on warm traffic from owned channels.

## 6. Event announcements
- **Floor:** 8% registration-to-attendance for cold traffic.
- **Stretch:** 22% for owned-channel traffic; 40%+ for hackathons with prize pools.

*(Real benchmarks are segmented by ICP, traffic source, and time-of-quarter. This file is the simplified one-pager.)*
