# Uniswap — Real Examples (demo, paraphrased shapes)

A small library of in-voice content shapes that already work. Reference these for shape, not for content. These are paraphrased shapes for the demo, not lifted Uniswap copy.

## 1. X — single post, "the unexpected number"
> *"1,400+ unique v4 hooks have been deployed in the first six months. Most people building on the protocol today are people you've never heard of."*

## 2. X — thread opener (tweet 1 of 7)
> *"A hook is not a smart contract. It's a callback at a known point in the swap path. That distinction changes how you architect everything that follows. Thread."*

## 3. LinkedIn — institutional framing
> *"A DAO treasury earning yield is not a regulatory question yet. It will be. Here's what we're seeing in the conversations our delegates are having with counsel."*

## 4. Newsletter — opener
> *"Proposal 67 entered voting yesterday. It changes how protocol fees can be turned on, and it does so in a way that's narrower than the version that failed last quarter. Here's what's different."*

## 5. Landing page — hero (hook spotlight page)
> *"Hooks turn Uniswap v4 into a programmable liquidity layer for builders."*
>
> *"Run any logic — TWAMM, dynamic fees, on-chain limit orders — at the moment of a swap."*

## What to learn from these
- They open with a fact, a stance, or a specific scenario — never with a setup or a greeting.
- They use one number per piece. Not three.
- They sound like a protocol engineer texting another protocol engineer, or a DAO treasurer texting their counsel.
